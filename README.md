# Systemic Risk Analysis of NBFCs in India (Post-IL&FS Crisis)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes systemic risk in Indian Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) by examining how financial structure and growth dynamics influence risk behavior, especially after the IL&FS crisis (2018).

The study applies panel data econometrics to understand firm-level financial stability and risk exposure over time.

---

## Business Problem
The rapid expansion of NBFCs in India has raised concerns about financial fragility and systemic risk.

This project answers:
- Does rapid growth increase financial risk?
- How does leverage impact risk exposure?
- Did the IL&FS crisis change NBFC risk dynamics?

---

## Objectives
- Examine relationship between leverage and systemic risk  
- Analyze impact of growth on financial stability  
- Evaluate post-crisis structural changes in NBFC sector  

---

## Tools & Techniques
- **R (plm package)** – Panel Data Analysis  
- **Excel** – Data preparation  
- **Econometric Models**:
  - Fixed Effects Model  
  - Random Effects Model  
  - Hausman Test  
- **Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)**  

---

## Dataset
- **Companies Covered**:
  - Bajaj Finance  
  - Shriram Finance  
  - M&M Finance  
  - LIC Housing Finance  

- **Time Period**: 2017–2025  
- **Observations**: 36 firm-year data points  

---

## Methodology
- Constructed **Risk Index** (3-year rolling standard deviation of net profit)  
- Applied **Fixed Effects and Random Effects models**  
- Used **Hausman Test** to select best model  
- Applied **Robust Standard Errors**  
- Conducted **SEM analysis** for validation  

---

## Key Findings
- Growth significantly increases systemic risk  
- Leverage impact varies across firms (not uniform)  
- Post-IL&FS period shows structural shift in risk behavior  
- Firm-specific characteristics strongly influence risk  

---

## Insights
- Rapid expansion increases financial vulnerability  
- Risk is driven more by growth than leverage alone  
- Regulatory changes altered risk dynamics but did not remove vulnerabilities

## Conclusion
Systemic risk in NBFCs is influenced by growth strategies, financial structure, and post-crisis regulatory changes. Sustainable expansion and strong risk management are essential for maintaining financial stability.
